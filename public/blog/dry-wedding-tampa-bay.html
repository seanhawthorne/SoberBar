How to Throw a Dry Wedding in Tampa Bay

July 2026 • Sober Bar, Gulfport, FL

A dry wedding doesn't mean a boring wedding. In fact, some of the most memorable receptions in Tampa Bay are the ones where nobody needs a designated driver.

If you're planning a dry wedding in Tampa Bay, here's how to make it feel celebratory, inclusive, and completely unforgettable.

1. Build a Real Bar Experience

The bar is still the bar. Stock it with craft mocktails, sparkling options, fresh juices, shrubs, and botanical sodas. Hire bartenders who know how to shake, stir, smoke, and garnish like they're making cocktails — because they are.

2. Create a Signature Mocktail

Give guests something to remember: a custom zero-proof drink named after you, your pet, or your first date spot. Serve it in proper glassware with a garnish that photographs well.

3. Offer Variety

Not everyone wants the same thing. A great dry bar includes:

  • Zero-proof cocktails
  • Kava or botanical drinks
  • Sparkling water and fancy sodas
  • Fresh coffee and espresso options
  • Seasonal shrubs and house-made syrups

4. Set the Mood

Alcohol isn't what makes a party. The music, lighting, food, dancing, and energy do. Invest in a great DJ or band, beautiful decor, and late-night snacks. Your guests won't miss the booze.

5. Communicate Early

Let guests know on the invitation or wedding website that the event will be alcohol-free. Frame it positively: "Join us for a celebration full of great food, dancing, and craft mocktails."
