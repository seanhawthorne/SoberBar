What Is Kava? A Beginner's Guide

July 2026 • Sober Bar, Gulfport, FL

If you've walked into Sober Bar and seen "kava" on the menu, you might be wondering: what is kava, and why is everyone talking about it?

Kava is a plant native to the South Pacific. For centuries, island communities have used it in social and ceremonial gatherings. Today, it's showing up in bars, cafes, and wellness spots across the U.S. — including right here in Gulfport, Florida.

What Is Kava Made From?

Kava comes from the root of the Piper methysticum plant. The root is ground into a powder, mixed with water, and strained to make a drink. Traditionally, it's consumed from a coconut shell or shared bowl in a group setting.

What Does Kava Feel Like?

Most people describe kava as calming and grounding. Effects can include:

  • A relaxed, mellow mood
  • Reduced social tension
  • A slight numbing or tingling sensation on the tongue
  • Clarity without drowsiness

Unlike alcohol, kava doesn't impair coordination or leave you with a hangover. That makes it popular with people who want a social, relaxing drink without the downsides.

What Does Kava Taste Like?

Traditional kava has an earthy, peppery flavor. Some people love it; others need a minute to get used to it. At Sober Bar, we blend kava into cocktails with coconut, citrus, ginger, and other flavors to make it more approachable.

Is Kava Safe?

Kava has been used safely in Pacific cultures for generations. As with any botanical, moderation matters. Kava may not be right for everyone, especially people with liver conditions or those taking certain medications. If you're unsure, check with your healthcare provider.
