Things to Do in Gulfport, Florida

A local's guide to the best eats, drinks, shops, and sunsets in Gulfport — without leaving Beach Boulevard.

Start with a Drink (Zero-Proof, of Course)

Sober Bar is Gulfport's first alcohol-free cocktail bar and coffee house. Grab a Smoked Old Fashioned, Kava Cloud, or Espresso Martini, then walk everything else from here.

Explore the Waterfront

  • Gulfport Pier — One of the best sunset views in Pinellas County. Free, dog-friendly, and a 2-minute walk from Sober Bar.
  • Williams Pier — Great for fishing, people-watching, and live music on weekends.
  • Gulfport Beach — Small, quiet, and perfect for a low-key beach day.

Eat Local

  • O'Maddy's Bar & Grille — Waterfront casual dining with live music.
  • Backfin Blue Cafe — Local favorite for seafood and Southern comfort.
  • La Caponata — Authentic Italian in the heart of Gulfport.
  • Sea Dog Brewing / The Tides — Solid brunch and bay views.

Shop & Stroll

  • Gulfport Art Walk — First Friday and third Saturday of every month. Galleries, street vendors, live music.
  • Tuesday Fresh Market — Local produce, artisans, and food trucks.
  • Beach Bazaar — Vintage Gulfport gifts and beach gear.

Events Worth Planning Around

  • GeckoFest — Gulfport's biggest annual street festival.
  • Holidays in the Bungalows — Neighborhood home tour during the holidays.
  • 4th of July Fireworks — Waterfront fireworks display.

The Perfect Afternoon Itinerary

  1. Start at Sober Bar for a coffee or zero-proof cocktail.
  2. Walk the pier and catch the breeze.
  3. Browse the shops along Beach Boulevard.
  4. Grab dinner at a local restaurant.
  5. End with a sunset mocktail back at Sober Bar.

Visiting Gulfport?

Sober Bar is open Tuesday through Sunday at 3062 Beach Blvd S — right off the main strip. Stop in for a drink that won't slow down the rest of your night.
